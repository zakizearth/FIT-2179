How the GOAT finally won the World Cup

After 36 years of waiting, Argentina lifted the World Cup trophy in Qatar on 18 December 2022. This visualisation tells the story of how Lionel Messi led his nation from a shock opening defeat to the greatest triumph in Argentine football history — told entirely through data.

Global Map of Participants

Argentina played all 7 matches across just 3 stadiums in Qatar. The Lusail Iconic Stadium was their home ground — hosting 5 of their 7 matches including the group stage comeback against Mexico, the quarter final against the Netherlands, and the unforgettable Final against France.

The Ahmed bin Ali Stadium in Al Rayyan hosted the Round of 16 clash against Australia, where Messi produced one of the goals of the tournament with a clinical solo finish.

Stadium 974 — the only fully demountable stadium ever used at a World Cup — hosted the group stage win over Poland that confirmed Argentina's place in the knockout rounds as Group C winners.

Messi in Numbers

Consistent from Start to Finish

Messi contributed in 6 of Argentina's 7 matches, going scoreless only against Poland — where he still provided an assist. He scored in the group stage, the round of 16, the quarter final, semi final, and the final itself.

His most impactful matches were the Netherlands quarter final (2 goals including a penalty) and the Final against France (2 goals — an early penalty and a stunning volley to make it 3–2).

No other player in World Cup history has scored in as many stages of a single tournament.

Outperforming Expectations

Expected Goals (xG) is a statistical measure of shot quality — it estimates how many goals a player "should" score based on where and how they shot. A value greater than xG means the player outperformed expectations.

Messi's total xG for the tournament was 6.6, yet he scored 7 goals — finishing above expectation. The chart shows this match by match: the dashed line is xG, the solid line is actual goals.

He was at his most clinical in the knockout rounds, where it mattered most.

The Team

Argentina's Team Performance

Argentina's triumph was not just about Messi. They were the most dominant team in the tournament — maintaining 80%+ passing accuracy in every single match, conceding just 8 goals across 7 games, and scoring 15 in total. The charts below show how their performance evolved across the tournament.

Goals Scored vs Conceded

Argentina scored in every match. Their only loss came in the group stage against Saudi Arabia. By the semi final they were dominant.

Possession & Pass Accuracy

Argentina dominated the ball in 5 of 7 matches. Pass accuracy never dropped below 80%, peaking at 90.8% against Poland.

Total Shots vs Shots on Target

Shot volume was highest against Poland (22 shots) and France (19 shots). Their conversion rate was especially strong in the knockout rounds.
